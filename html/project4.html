1. Nepal, India

Nepal is a landlocked South Asian country famous for the Himalayas, including Mount Everest, boasting immense geographical diversity from plains to high peaks, alongside rich cultural tapestry, multi-ethnic groups, Hinduism and Buddhism, with Kathmandu as its capital and a growing tourism sector

2. Egypt, Northeast Africa

Egypt is a transcontinental country in Northeast Africa, famed for its ancient civilization centered on the Nile River, home to pyramids and hieroglyphs, with a modern Arab culture, Islamic majority, and a strategic location connecting Africa and Asia, though much of its land is desert, with most life concentrated along the fertile Nile Valley and Delta.

3. Niagara Falls

Niagara Falls is a world-renowned collection of three massive waterfalls—Horseshoe Falls, American Falls, and Bridal Veil Falls—located on the Niagara River bordering Ontario, Canada, and New York, USA. Formed ~12,000 years ago, it boasts a high flow rate of over 2,800 cubic meters per second, drawing millions of tourists annually for its immense power, beauty, and boat tours like the Maid of the Mist.
